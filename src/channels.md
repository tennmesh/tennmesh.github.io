  <div class="content-section">
    <h1>Recommended Hashtag Channels</h1>
    <p>
      Hashtag channels make it easy to organize conversations around specific topics on the network. By starting a channel name with a <code>#</code>, MeshCore automatically generates the channel key, allowing anyone using the same channel name to join and communicate. These channels are great for coordinating around particular subjects or local groups.
    </p>
  </div>

  <div class="content-section">
    <h2>How to Join a Hashtag Channel</h2>
    <p>Joining a hashtag channel is easy. Follow these steps in the MeshCore app:</p>
    <ol class="join-steps">
      <li>Tap on the <strong>3-dot Menu</strong></li>
      <li>Select <strong>Add Channel</strong></li>
      <li>Choose <strong>Join a Hashtag Channel</strong></li>
      <li>Enter the <strong>Channel Name</strong></li>
      <li>Tap <strong>Join Channel</strong></li>
      <li>Select <strong>Continue to Channel</strong></li>
      <li>Start Chatting!</li>
    </ol>
  </div>

  <div class="content-section">
    <h2>Recommended Channels</h2>
    <p>
      Below are some commonly used and recommended hashtag channels on TennMesh to help keep conversations organized across the network. These channels make it easier for users to find discussions related to specific topics while keeping the main public channel from becoming too cluttered.
    </p>

    <h3 class="channel-group-label">General</h3>
    <div class="channel-list">
      <div class="channel-card">
        <div class="channel-name">#test</div>
        <p>The recommended channel for testing your devices and seeing if others copy you.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="channel-card">
        <div class="channel-name">#bot</div>
        <p>The recommended channel for automated bots.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="channel-card">
        <div class="channel-name">#tenntalk</div>
        <p>General chat for Tennessee. Think of this as a Tennessee-specific "Public" channel.</p>
      </div>
    </div>

    <h3 class="channel-group-label">Tennessee Regions</h3>
    <p class="channel-group-desc">Tennessee is divided into three regions: East, Middle, and West. Separate hashtag channels are available for each region so users can easily coordinate, share updates, and communicate with others nearby.</p>
    <div class="channel-list">
      <div class="channel-card">
        <div class="channel-name">#tn-east</div>
        <p>Conversations and coordination for East Tennessee.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="channel-card">
        <div class="channel-name">#tn-middle</div>
        <p>Conversations and coordination for Middle Tennessee.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="channel-card">
        <div class="channel-name">#tn-west</div>
        <p>Conversations and coordination for West Tennessee.</p>
      </div>
    </div>

    <h3 class="channel-group-label">County Channels</h3>
    <p class="channel-group-desc">County-specific channels follow the <code>#tn-[county]</code> format for consistency. Since Tennessee has 95 counties, not all are listed here but any county can create their own channel using this naming convention.</p>
    <div class="channel-list">
      <div class="channel-card channel-card--example">
        <div class="channel-name">#tn-knox</div>
        <p>Local channel for Knox County.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="channel-card channel-card--example">
        <div class="channel-name">#tn-mcminn</div>
        <p>Local channel for McMinn County.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="channel-card channel-card--example">
        <div class="channel-name">#tn-polk</div>
        <p>Local channel for Polk County.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
    <div class="note">
      <p>The examples above show the recommended format. Use <code>#tn-[countyname]</code> to create or join a channel for your county.</p>
    </div>
